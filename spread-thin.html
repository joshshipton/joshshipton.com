You should be doing more, especially if you’re average.

I didn’t feel like writing a blog post today but my friend Zac told me “Josh you haven't written a blog post in a while you should write one” So here it is. Now a little bit about me to give context for this rant. I’m a pretty average individual. I’ve never been really amazing at anything but I’ve worked hard to become almost-decent in a few domains and something I’ve figured out is that the best way to stand-out when you’re average is to spread yourself thin. You should be dedicating your time to at least two pursuits even if totally unrelated.

The reason for this.

It’s really hard to stand out in a single domain. Being the best at anything is extremely difficult and in today's world of instant, wide-reaching comparison you’re always going to be compared to the best. But if you spread yourself thin, it becomes vastly easier to

  1. Be perceived as someone who is good at something and
  2. Mediate your self worth

On point 1. be a bear on a bicycle.

No I’ve not gone crazy, this means something. I got it from Chris Williams and what it means is that individually both of these things are pretty standard. You see a bear. That's standard. You see a bicycle and even more so. But a bear ON a bicycle. Now that's something special. You can use this principle in your own life to stand out, a CEO who writes amazing poetry. Wow. An athlete who’s also a brilliant writer. Wow. A successful lawyer who can also play high level league of legends... wow?? (ok maybe not that last one).

On point 2.

I know people like to say that their self-worth isn't tied to anything but rather an unchanging property that’s immune to any outside influences. But at the end of the day a harsh truth is that other people will judge you not only on your character but also on your capacity as a human. If you think they don’t look at how people treat a CEO of a successful company compared to a homeless man. Splitting yourself across two domains lets you lean on one when the other isn’t going great. Injured from your sport, go code. Can’t act much at the moment because there are no productions? Go write another novel. It decreases the chance that you can’t perform and bring some kind of value to the world or to yourself, reducing the chance of your sense of worth being affected.

How I apply this to my own life.

Personally I split most of my time between the Olympic sport of Judo and programming with a ton of small interests scattered into the mix (chess, studying german, reading, writing, etc, etc). I'm pretty average at both (if you don’t know what Judo is watch this ). But if you compare me to other programmers I’m probably an above average athlete. And if you compare me to other athletes I'm probably an above average programmer. More than that I also personally enjoy being multifaceted. It means that I’m around a more diverse range of people and get to switch between doing vastly different things on a daily basis. Other than that I feel like being a well rounded human is just a nice thing to do. You should read Range if you like this idea but want actual logical reasons for being a multi-dimensional human being. It's a really good read where the author gives a scientific account against early specialization and advocates for a broad range of experience.
